## Programming

### Getting Started
- **[freeCodeCamp](https://www.freecodecamp.org/)** — Free, project-based courses in web development, Python, data science, and more.
- **[The Odin Project](https://www.theodinproject.com/)** — Full-stack web development curriculum, entirely free and open source.
- **[CS50 (Harvard)](https://cs50.harvard.edu/x/)** — Harvard's legendary introduction to computer science, free to audit.

### Python
- **[Python.org Tutorial](https://docs.python.org/3/tutorial/)** — The official Python tutorial, great for beginners.
- **[Automate the Boring Stuff with Python](https://automatetheboringstuff.com/)** — Free book teaching practical Python through real-world tasks.
- **[Real Python](https://realpython.com/)** — Tutorials and articles ranging from beginner to advanced Python.

### Web Development
- **[MDN Web Docs](https://developer.mozilla.org/)** — The definitive reference for HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
- **[javascript.info](https://javascript.info/)** — Modern, thorough JavaScript tutorial.
- **[CSS-Tricks](https://css-tricks.com/)** — Tips, tricks, and tutorials for CSS.

### Data & Machine Learning
- **[Kaggle Learn](https://www.kaggle.com/learn)** — Free, hands-on courses in Python, SQL, machine learning, and data visualization.
- **[fast.ai](https://www.fast.ai/)** — Practical deep learning for coders, free and beginner-friendly.
- **[Google's Machine Learning Crash Course](https://developers.google.com/machine-learning/crash-course)** — Free intro to ML from Google.

### Practice & Challenges
- **[LeetCode](https://leetcode.com/)** — Algorithm and data structure practice problems.
- **[HackerRank](https://www.hackerrank.com/)** — Coding challenges across many languages and domains.
- **[Exercism](https://exercism.org/)** — Coding exercises with mentor feedback, free.
- **[Codewars](https://www.codewars.com/)** — Community-driven coding challenges at varying difficulty levels.

## Mathematics

### General
- **[Khan Academy — Math](https://www.khanacademy.org/math)** — Free, adaptive math lessons from arithmetic through calculus.
- **[Paul's Online Math Notes](https://tutorial.math.lamar.edu/)** — Excellent free notes for Algebra, Calculus I/II/III, and Differential Equations.
- **[Wolfram Alpha](https://www.wolframalpha.com/)** — Powerful computational engine for checking work and exploring concepts.

### Calculus & Beyond
- **[3Blue1Brown — Essence of Calculus](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDMsr9K-rj53DwVRMYO3t5Yr)** — Beautiful visual introduction to calculus on YouTube.
- **[MIT OpenCourseWare — Mathematics](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/mathematics/)** — Free course materials from MIT's mathematics department.

### Statistics
- **[StatQuest with Josh Starmer](https://www.youtube.com/@statquest)** — Statistics and machine learning explained visually and clearly on YouTube.
- **[OpenIntro Statistics](https://www.openintro.org/book/os/)** — Free, open-source introductory statistics textbook.

## Writing & Research

### Writing
- **[Purdue OWL](https://owl.purdue.edu/)** — Comprehensive resource for grammar, citation styles (APA, MLA, Chicago), and academic writing.
- **[Hemingway Editor](https://hemingwayapp.com/)** — Free browser-based tool to improve clarity and readability of your writing.
- **[Grammarly](https://www.grammarly.com/)** — Grammar and style checker with a generous free tier.

### Research & Citations
- **[Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/)** — Free academic search engine for scholarly articles and papers.
- **[Zotero](https://www.zotero.org/)** — Free, open-source reference manager to collect, organize, and cite research.
- **[Semantic Scholar](https://www.semanticscholar.org/)** — AI-powered research tool for finding and understanding academic papers.

## Study Skills & Productivity

### Learning Techniques
- **[Learning How to Learn (Coursera)](https://www.coursera.org/learn/learning-how-to-learn)** — Free course on the science of learning; one of the most popular MOOCs ever.
- **[Anki](https://apps.ankiweb.net/)** — Free spaced-repetition flashcard app — the most effective way to memorize information long-term.

### Note-Taking & Organization
- **[Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/)** — Free, local-first note-taking app with powerful linking between notes.
- **[Notion](https://www.notion.so/students)** — All-in-one workspace for notes, tasks, and projects; free for students.

### Time Management
- **[Pomodoro Technique](https://todoist.com/productivity-methods/pomodoro-technique)** — Study in focused 25-minute blocks with short breaks; great for avoiding burnout.
- **[Todoist](https://todoist.com/)** — Simple, powerful task manager with a generous free plan.

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of These Resources

1. **Don't just read — build things.** Apply what you learn immediately in small projects.
2. **Use spaced repetition.** Review material over increasing intervals with Anki or similar tools.
3. **Teach it.** Explaining a concept to someone else (or even to yourself out loud) reveals gaps in your understanding.
4. **Be consistent over intense.** 30 minutes of focused study every day beats a 5-hour marathon once a week.
5. **Ask questions.** Stack Overflow, Reddit communities (r/learnprogramming, r/learnmath), and Discord servers are full of helpful people.
